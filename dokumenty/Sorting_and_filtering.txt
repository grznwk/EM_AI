Sorting and filtering products

Products in the table can be sorted ascending or descending by most columns.
  
You can also filter products by values from individual columns. 
Depending on the nature of the column, the filter can be a simple text filter (e.g., set on the product name), or be of a completely different nature. 
For example, in the case of filtering by ETIM classes, it will be a selection of one or more values.
And in the case of last modification date, it can be a selection with an indication of the last period or date range.

As you can see, the filter can be built on any number of columns. 
All criteria from each column must be met at the same time - only such products are displayed.

You can also use the side panel to manage filters.

Using the side panel, you can also add filtering on individual columns, including on columns that are not currently visible in the table. 
 

Deleting filtering criteria related to individual columns can be done in a form accessible from the column or from the side panel.
  
The created filter can be saved under a name of your choice, allowing you to disable filtering and return to the selected filtering criteria at any time. 
You can also switch between different saved filters.
  
The current list of all saved fixed filters is available in the menu: Configuration / Filters. 
Here you can edit the name of the filter or delete it.
