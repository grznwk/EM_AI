We’ll explain you how to use labels in the ETIM-Mapper system. 
Labels help you manage product information faster.

They improve teamwork.
And they help you prepare your offer for sales. 
Labels are color-coded letters.
They carry specific meanings.

You can assign them to individual products.

Like colorful sticky notes that can be attached to a product.
  
They help you categorize and filter products.

They make searching easier.

They support product management.

And they help with integration to other systems.

Labels let you group products by custom features.

Features that don’t always fit standard categories.

For example:
You can label products as “Ready for publication”, 
“Needs translation”,
or “Update photos”.

This makes it easier to control data quality and plan your team’s next steps.

Let’s get started.

Go to the main menu.

Click Configuration.

Then, click Labels.
 
This is where you manage your labels.

To create a new label, click the plus icon.

Now define the label:
Add a unique ID. 
A name.
A description. 
Pick a color.
And choose a letter.

Labels help group products that share a trait.

No need to search the entire database.

It’s great for tasks like updating descriptions, adding images, or exporting selected products to partners.

Let’s create a label for products that need checking.

Set the ID to “2Bchecked”.
Pick orange. 
Choose the letter C.
Add the description: “To be checked” and save.
  
Now let’s make one for new products.
ID: “new”. 
Color: green.
Letter: N. 
Description: “New product”.
And save.
  
Now let’s assign labels to products.
Go to the All Products view.

Select some of them.
Use the Bulk Edit form.
 

Select the orange label C.
Click OK. 
Click on “Modify” and confirm.

Done!

The selected products are now labeled with C.
  
Let’s do the same for some new products.
Select a few products.

Click Modify under the Labels column.
Choose the green label N.

Click OK, Modify and confirm the modification.
 
Now those products are labeled as new.
  
You can now see these labels attached to products.

It's easy to spot products that are new,
or that need to be reviewed.

Labels also work with filters.

In the label management screen you can mark labels as favorites.

Favorite labels are always displayed at the top of the list, making them easy to select, especially when there are many labels.

User-created labels are rectangular.

You can edit or delete them.

If you delete a label, 
it disappears from all products it was assigned to.

All filters containing deleted labels are also deleted.

Round labels are permanent (system) labels.

They can’t be changed.
They have special meanings.

For example:

“Virtual” marks abstract products, representing, for example, a certain category of products.

“Public” label marks products that are visible for external users, without logging in.

To sum up:

Labels make your product management easier, clearer, and more organized.

Use them to stay in control — and move faster as a team.
